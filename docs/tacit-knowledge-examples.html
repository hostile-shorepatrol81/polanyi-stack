Tacit knowledge examples

Tacit knowledge shows up wherever the difference between a beginner and an expert cannot be explained by access to the same explicit instructions.

Home GitHub

Tacit knowledge in code review

A strong reviewer often notices trust boundaries, weird side effects, or design smells before they can fully articulate them. That is tacit knowledge.

Tacit knowledge in onboarding

New hires may read every document and still not know what to pay attention to in a live task. That missing layer is usually cue recognition and boundary judgment.

Tacit knowledge in diagnosis

Whether in medicine, debugging, or operations, experts pick up weak signals early and know when to zoom out. That attention structure is rarely captured by a checklist alone.

Tacit knowledge in design critique and sales

Design reviewers and strong sales operators often have reliable taste and timing but weak explicit narration. They know what feels wrong, what feels thin, and when a line of action should stop.
